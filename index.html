ABOUT · HOW THIS COURSE WORKS

Watch, read, play, quiz — in that order

This course is a series of short videos (hosted on 網路大學, the university e-learning site) paired with Jupyter notebooks and RISE slides. Each module below has a companion page — a guided version of the material with video timelines, worked digests, interactive widgets, a self-check quiz from the course test bank, and key-term flashcards.

One thing this course is not: software-engineering training. Think of it as a toolbox for the subjects you are already studying — when calculus hands you an integral, linear algebra a matrix, or statistics a claim about data, you will be able to compute it, draw it, and verify your own answer. A result you have checked with your own hands is a result you truly understand.

📖 THE STUDY LOOP

① Watch one video on 網路大學 → ② Re-run the matching notebook section yourself (Colab or Jupyter) → ③ Play with the widgets on the companion page until the concept feels obvious → ④ Quiz yourself and flip the flashcards at the end of each module.

🎒 PREREQUISITES

Basic Python: variables, flow control, functions, containers, and a nodding acquaintance with OOP vocabulary (class, object, method, attribute). The modules are designed for first- and second-year college students and reinforce linear algebra, calculus, and statistics along the way.

Need a refresher first? Python for Everybody · Automate the Boring Stuff with Python · Python Crash Course

🤖 STUDY WITH AN AI

Every module page includes "Think · Discuss · Ask an AI" prompts. Two habits that make AI a study partner instead of an answer machine:

Before asking: write down your own prediction. Then ask the AI and compare — where you disagree is where you learn. After a quiz mistake: paste the question and YOUR wrong answer, and ask the AI to explain why the right one wins — not just what it is.
